Software & Tools

Q13: How do I use Excel to perform a two-sided t-test?

In Excel, you can calculate the p-value for a two-tailed t-test using:

  • p-value = 2 × T.DIST(−t, df, TRUE) (lower tail method)
  • p-value = 2 × [1 − T.DIST(t, df, TRUE)] (upper tail method)

For a negative t-value, the lower tail method is straightforward: p-value = 2 × T.DIST(t, df, TRUE).

Data & Analysis Concepts

Q20: Why do we need Identifier Variables in data analysis?

Identifier Variables uniquely identify each row in a dataset and play a crucial role in data integrity and quality control. While they are not used in analysis, they help in:

  • Tracking and verifying records
  • Maintaining anonymity in research
  • Cross-referencing and resolving discrepancies

It is correct to say that Identifier Variables are categorical, where the number of categories equals the total number of records.
